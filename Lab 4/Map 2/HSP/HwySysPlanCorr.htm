Title: WSDOT Highway System Plan (HSP) Corridors 2014
Geospatial_Data_Presentation_Form: vector digital data
Description:
Abstract:
Corridor Sketch The WSDOT Highway System Plan (HSP) Corridors 2014, Corridor Limits layer uses vector data to display linear features of the corridors. The feature was created using the increasing State Route (SR) Accumulated Route Mile (ARM) values. Corridor Identification and Establishment Considerations WSDOT Planners divided or segmented the state's highway system into "Functional Corridors" that serve/support communities, economic regions and regional networks. The following areas were considered as part of this segmentation process:
Traffic Characteristics ADT; Traffic Types; Commuter, Recreational, Seasonal; Freight Classification & Origins/Destinations.
Roadway Characteristics Change in number of Travel Lanes/Traffic Volume; Seasonal Closures (Mountain Passes); Coincidental Routes.
Access Connections to WSF/International Ferries; Connections to Transit/Passenger Rail/Multimodal Terminals; Connections to Sea/Air Ports; Connections to Rail/Truck Distribution Centers; Ports of Entry (border crossings); Access Management.
Roadway Classifications State Functional Classification; National Highway System designation; FGTS designation.
Land Use Characteristics Urban/Rural; Commercial/Industrial; Retail; Agricultural; Urban Growth Area Boundaries.
Other Major Traffic Generators Military Bases; Health Care complexes,; Recreational sites.
Proximity/Availability of Alternate Transportation Facilities Parallel roadways; Multimodal alternatives.
Corridor Length It may be useful to split long corridors, even though they may be fairly uniform in physical and travel characteristics.
Purpose:
The Corridor Sketch data is used by statewide planners and others to support the development of the Washington State Department of Transportation (WSDOT) Highway System Plan (HSP). The HSP includes an assessment of the state-owned transportation facilities and assets. This assessment is facilitated through programmatic and corridor approaches (Corridor Sketch Initiative).
The Corridor Sketch Initiative (CSI) is a new way for the Washington State Department of Transportation to work jointly with partners to capture and document consistent baseline information about each transportation corridor around the state in order to inform future investment decisions. (for more information see <http://www.wsdot.wa.gov/planning/HSP>)
